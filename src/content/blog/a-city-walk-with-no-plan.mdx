The best city walks begin with a loose direction and no demand to be efficient.

Efficiency is useful when you are catching a train. It is less useful when you are trying to meet a place.

## Let the side street win

The main road tells you what the city wants visitors to see. The side street tells you how the city breathes.

That is where the bakery smell escapes, where chairs sit outside a repair shop, where a painted door makes you slow down for no logical reason.

## Take fewer photographs

I like taking photographs, but a walk can become thin if every moment is converted into proof.

Some scenes are better kept as memory. A photograph is not the only way to honor attention.

## Return differently

On the way back, choose another route. The same city becomes a second city. You notice how quickly certainty dissolves once you move one block away from the familiar path.

That is the gift of walking without a plan.
