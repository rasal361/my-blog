The best reading habit I know is not heroic. It is small enough to survive ordinary days.

Read a few pages in the morning. Keep a book where your phone usually waits. Stop forcing yourself through books that are not alive for you right now.

## Make the book visible

The book you can see is easier to begin. Put one on the desk, one near the bed, and one in your bag. This is not about owning many books. It is about removing the first point of friction.

## Read with a pencil

Writing in the margin changes the feeling of reading. You are no longer consuming a finished object. You are having a conversation.

Mark the lines that make you pause. Write the question before it disappears. Fold nothing sacred into the page. A book that has been lived with should look lived with.

## Keep a short record

After finishing a book, write five sentences:

- What stayed with me?
- What did I disagree with?
- What changed how I see something?
- Who should read this?
- What will I do differently?

That is enough. The habit is not the performance of having read. The habit is returning to attention.
